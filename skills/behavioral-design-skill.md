# Behavioral Design Skill

Purpose
- Provide a concise reference for behavioral design patterns, reframing techniques, and persuasive interventions that prioritize perception and meaning over purely technical fixes.

When to use
- Called by `agents/12-behavioral-reframe-agent.md` when assessing proposals, generating reframes, or designing low-cost experiments.

Core Mental Models
- Framing & Reframing
- Anchoring
- Endowment / Ownership
- Loss Aversion
- Default Effects
- Social Proof & Signalling
- Scarcity & Urgency
- Satisficing & Effort Minimisation
- Uncertainty Reduction
- Placebo / Perceived Quality

Reframe Checklist (quick)
- Who benefits from this change psychologically? (status, certainty, convenience)
- What small cue can change perception (copy, layout, micro-animation)?
- Can we test a default or anchor instead of rebuilding features?
- Is there a social signal we can surface (peer usage, endorsements)?
- What's the ethical boundary? Do we need explicit consent?

Common Patterns & Example Tactics
- "Make it feel owned": save starter content, pre-filled dashboards, visible progress
- "Anchor up": show a premium-priced reference to increase perceived value of main tier
- "Make waiting meaningful": reduce uncertainty (ETA), add narrative explaining the wait
- "Ceremony & scarcity": scheduled review slots, limited cohort access, publishable showcases
- "Visible safety": show audit badges and residency info to reduce sharing anxiety

Output Templates (quick snippets)
- Headline reframe: "From busy work to leadership stories — submit one line, we do the rest."
- Microcopy confirm: "We've pre-filled your KPI values from last quarter — review & confirm"
- Security badge text: "Audit-logged • Sweden-resident • Encrypted"

References
- Kahneman, Thinking, Fast and Slow (mental models summary)
- Thaler & Sunstein, Nudge (choice architecture)
- Sutherland, Alchemy (examples & mindset)
